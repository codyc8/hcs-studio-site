<section class="cs-body">
  <div class="grid">
    <div>
      <span class="eyebrow ey">§ Brief</span>
      <h2>The <span class="it">problem.</span></h2>
    </div>
    <div>
      <p>Robin's CEO was reviewing five tools every morning — a CRM, a payments processor, a ticketing tool, a marketing dashboard, and a homegrown spreadsheet — to answer one question: <em>are we okay this week?</em></p>
      <p>The answer was always there. It was just scattered across five logins and three time zones. We were asked to compress all of it into a single screen the executive team could open with a coffee.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="grid" style="margin-top: 80px;">
    <div>
      <span class="eyebrow ey">§ Approach</span>
      <h2>One <span class="it">screen.</span> Five sources.</h2>
    </div>
    <div>
      <p>We started with a 90-minute working session — the CEO walked us through the actual morning routine, tab by tab. We grouped the questions into four blocks: <em>revenue</em>, <em>pipeline</em>, <em>operations</em>, and <em>customer health</em>. Each block became a card, each card a real-time read from the underlying source.</p>
      <p>The hard part wasn't visualization — it was reconciliation. Two of the five tools disagreed on what counted as a "customer." We built a thin reconciliation layer in the middle so the dashboard always told one story, even when the upstream tools didn't.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="grid" style="margin-top: 80px;">
    <div>
      <span class="eyebrow ey">§ Outcome</span>
      <h2>Mornings, <span class="it">solved.</span></h2>
    </div>
    <div>
      <p>The Robin executive team now spends roughly six minutes on the morning review, down from forty. The dashboard runs as a live web app the team opens in a browser tab; we host it, monitor it, and ship updates as the underlying tools change.</p>
      <p>Six months in, we've added two more sources — a recruiting pipeline and a usage telemetry feed — without touching the front-end design. That's the point: the screen stays calm even as the business grows behind it.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
